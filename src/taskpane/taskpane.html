Variance Analysis Compare years & flag anomalies RUN
What it checksCompares this year against last year line by line across the Income Statement, Balance Sheet and Cash Flow, flags unusual movements, and runs tie-out checks (does the balance sheet balance? does cash tie across statements?) plus cross-statement ratios.
What it needsAt least one worksheet named IS/Income Statement, BS/Balance Sheet or CF/Cash Flow — it analyses whichever it finds.
Set a materiality thresholdBefore it runs, you set a clearly-trivial threshold: line items whose change is smaller than this are set aside as trivial, so you focus on the moves that matter. A sensible default (≈ 1% of the largest figure) is pre-filled — overwrite it if you like.
What you getA year-on-year table per statement, tie-out results and ratios, plus suggested questions to put to the CFO. Click any table row to highlight its source cells in Excel.

DCF Valuation Build a discounted cash flow model RUN
What it buildsTwo new sheets: a WACC tab (CAPM build-up) and a DCF tab — driver-based forecast, discounting, terminal value, and the bridge to an implied share price. Every assumption stays an editable cell.
What it needsAt least the past 3 years of historicals on worksheets named IS/Income Statement and CF/Cash Flow. A BS tab is optional (auto-locates cash and debt).
You approve the assumptionsThe AI suggests WACC inputs, terminal growth and the forecast drivers — each with a stated rationale — and nothing is written until you approve the card.
